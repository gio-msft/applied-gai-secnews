🧩 **Securing LLM Agents Need Intent-to-Execution Integrity** [source](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.16976v1.pdf) #security `7/10` 

 *Wenjie Qu, Ming Xu, Peiran Wang et al. (National University of Singapore, University of California, Los Angeles, University of California, Berkeley)*

 The key move is treating LLM agents like compilers and defining a semantic-preservation-style integrity spec that exposes why today’s prompt-injection and sandbox defenses don’t compose into end-to-end security.
 - Open, third‑party tool ecosystems break the “trusted tools” assumption and make supply‑chain risk a first-class threat for LLM agents, with reported evidence including >1,000 malicious skills planted in one campaign and 26.1% of 42,447 audited skills containing at least one vulnerability.
 - End-to-end agent security can be framed as “intent-to-execution integrity,” requiring four integrity conditions to hold simultaneously—Instruction, Data Flow, Judgment, and Tool Integrity—because any mis-execution maps to corruption of reasoning, action construction, or tool execution.
 - Mapping existing defenses to these four properties shows coverage is partial and non-compositional (strongest on constraining tool-call construction, weakest on reasoning robustness and behavioral conformance of tools), implying defense stacking without a shared correctness target will systematically leave exploitable gaps.

🕸️ **GRID: Graph Representation of Intelligence Data for Security Text Knowledge Graph Construction** [source](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.16714v1.pdf) #cyber `7/10` 

 *Liangyi Huang, Zichen Liu, Fei Shao et al. (Arizona State University, Case Western Reserve University, University of Notre Dame, TensorBlock, Facebook)*

 Turning open-ended CTI document-to-knowledge-graph extraction into cheap, reusable edge-auditable training tasks yields higher recall and similar top-line F1 at a fraction of the token and reward-computation cost.
 - A 4B Qwen3-based CTI graph extractor trained with an offline task-bank reward plus ontology-guided inference achieved 84.62% average precision, 64.91% average recall, and 68.53% average F1 across 249 CTI articles from five datasets, delivering the highest recall among compared systems while staying essentially tied for best F1.

 *Chenwang Wu, Yiuming Cheung, Bo Han et al. (Hong Kong Baptist University, South China University of Technology, University of Science and Technology of China, State Key Laboratory of Cognitive Intelligence)*

 Treating per-token detector scores as a structured sequence—then calibrating locally and reasoning globally—turns fragile metric-based MGT detection into a far more transferable and low-overhead detector stack.
 - Model-agnostic, metric-based MGT detectors share a common failure mode where token-level scores are biased by sampling randomness and simple averaging/thresholding cannot correct this, making token-score precision the main bottleneck for text-level detection.

 *Zelin Zhang, Qi Li, Jie Cao et al. (Queen’s University, University of Waterloo)*

 The most striking shift is that LLM outputs are increasingly executable actions, and the measured success rates for tool poisoning and indirect prompt injection indicate that capability gains can directly amplify compromise risk.
 - As generative systems shift from producing content to executing tool-backed actions, the attack surface escalates from single-session prompt injection to cross-system agentic exploit chains, and early evidence already shows tool poisoning reaching 72.8% attack success on real-world MCP servers.

 - Model integrity is fragile across the lifecycle: as few as 250 poison samples can backdoor models across 600M–13B scales and safety fine-tuning removal is reported at under $200, implying that alignment and supply-chain controls must be treated as first-class security requirements.

🔐 **Backchaining Loss of Control Mitigations from Mission-Specific Benchmarks in National Security** [source](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.21095v1.pdf) #security `6/10` 

 *Matteo Pistillo, Samantha Faraone, Joshua Herman (Apollo Research, U.S. Department of the Treasury)*

 Benchmark mistakes can be treated as a permissions audit: remove the minimal access that makes the wrong answer dangerous, without crippling the right answer.
 - A practical three-step workflow is introduced to mitigate AI Loss-of-Control risk by (1) evaluating models on mission-specific national-security benchmarks, (2) backchaining from statistically significant wrong answers to the exact affordances/permissions that would enable downstream harm, and (3) selectively removing only those access rights not needed for the correct behavior.
 - In a derivative-classification case study, restricting two specific permissions—editing source notes to add markings and summarizing from unmarked lines—blocks the main harmful failure modes (over-classification and unauthorized disclosure) while preserving the intended safe workflow of proposing markings with confidence and routing for human review.
 - The method operationalizes least-privilege for AI agents by turning benchmark errors into concrete access-control changes, shifting LoC preparedness from abstract threat mapping to deployer-generated, auditable mitigations that can be implemented immediately in high-stakes environments.

📉 **On the Geometric Limits of Transformer Defenses against Obfuscation Attacks: Latent Embedding Collapse & Performance Robustness Gap** [source](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.19159v1.pdf) #security `6/10` 

 *Becky Mashaido, Tapadhir Das (University of the Pacific)*

 Detectors can look “solved” at ~99% F1 while obfuscated prompts still collapse into the clean embedding manifold (δ≈1), meaning accuracy is a poor proxy for representational robustness against prompt obfuscation.
 - Across DistilBERT, BERT-Base, and BERT-Medium, 4-way prompt-injection detectors sustain near-identical ~99.3–99.4% accuracy/F1, yet the clean–obfuscated minimum embedding margin is only δ=1.02, showing that near-perfect detection can coexist with near-overlap in latent space.
 - Obfuscated prompts produce severe latent instability despite correct classification, with obfuscated intra-class variance reaching 3.33±6.23 and a high-variance clean–obfuscated mean distance of 24.34±4.51, implying the representation is geometrically fragile under multi-operator Unicode/zero-width/noise perturbations.
 - Scaling encoder depth/capacity (DistilBERT → BERT-Base/Medium) does not eliminate partial manifold overlap in PCA/t-SNE projections, indicating a persistent performance–robustness gap that motivates adding geometry-aware robustness metrics alongside standard precision/recall evaluations.

🕸️ **Trustworthy Agent Network: Trust in Agent Networks Must Be Baked In, Not Bolted On** [source](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.19035v1.pdf) #security `6/10` 

 *Yixiang Yao, Yuhang Yao, Xinyi Fan et al. (University of Southern California, Carnegie Mellon University, University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, Stanford University)*

 The key insight is treating multi-agent trust as a state-transition invariant—if unsafe states are reachable, no amount of after-the-fact monitoring can make an open agent ecosystem reliably safe.
 - Agent-to-Agent (A2A) networks introduce compositional failure modes—adversarial composition, semantic misalignment, cascading errors, privacy re-identification, and non-terminating loops—that remain reachable even when each individual agent is locally aligned or protocol-compliant.
 - Bolted-on trust mechanisms (guardrails, LLM-judges, post-hoc verification) leave the underlying network transition dynamics unconstrained, so safety depends on monitor reliability and can still be bypassed via false negatives, delay, or compliant-but-dangerous messages.
 - Baked-in trust is framed as constraining the network transition function so unsafe states are unreachable by construction, operationalizing trust via four pillars (compositional robustness, semantic containment, accountability/attribution, and cross-boundary reliability) and tracking costs with latency, overhead, scalability, and determinism metrics.

⏳ **Surviving the Unseen: Predictive Defense for Novel Multi-Turn Multimodal Attacks** [source](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.18988v1.pdf) #security `6/10` 

 *Doohee You (Google)*

 Treating multimodal jailbreak defense as survival prediction turns gradual, cross-turn intent smuggling into an estimable “time-to-failure” signal rather than a brittle per-turn classification problem.
 - Static, per-turn (Markovian) guardrails are positioned as structurally insufficient for multi-turn multimodal attacks because adversaries can distribute intent below single-turn thresholds, requiring trajectory-level detection that accumulates evidence over time.
 - TRIAD proposes a trigger-based cascade—Isolation Forest as a low-cost scout followed by Ledoit–Wolf-regularized Mahalanobis drift and second-derivative “trajectory acceleration” features—so expensive high-dimensional checks run only when early anomaly signals fire.

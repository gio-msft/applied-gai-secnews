 A modular LangGraph/PyRIT/DSPy pipeline shows prompt optimization can cut real multi-turn behavioral jailbreak rates, but it cannot compensate for weak agent routing and reasoning architecture.
 - Across 40 trials per persona with multi-turn adaptive attacks, DSPy prompt optimization reduced attacker success on higher-level behavioral manipulation—Out-of-Scope (TOP-1) by 29 percentage points (84%→55%), Competitor Probing (TOP-3) by 14 pp (87%→73%), and Format Sabotage (REC-3) by 13 pp (33%→20%)—showing prompt-hardening can materially improve multi-agent refusal persistence and policy adherence.
 - Direct security exploits (prompt/system-prompt extraction and credential/API key demands) achieved 0% attacker success in both base and optimized prompts, indicating a strong baseline prompt+model can fully shut down common “ask for secrets” attacks but also creates a ceiling that hides whether optimizers add value on these vectors.
 - Prompt-only hardening hit architectural limits: attacks routed to a recommender sub-agent without an intermediate reasoning layer remained vulnerable (e.g., Vague Location REC-2 showed no improvement and residual failures persisted in TOP-3), implying robust defenses require routing/tool-call enforcement and not just better system prompts.

🗳️ **The Almost Intelligent Revolution: Options for Scaling Up Deliberation and Empowering People with AI** [source](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.19864v1.pdf) #security `6/10` 

 *Serge Sharoff (University of Leeds)*

 LLMs can scale and translate deliberation across registers to reduce linguistic exclusion, but their bias, sycophancy, and acceptance penalties can systematically steer outcomes and shrink participation among sceptical groups.
 - In a 5,734-person UK study of AI-mediated deliberation, participants preferred AI-generated group statements over human mediators on quality, clarity, informativeness, and fairness, and the AI increased within-group consensus but tended to shift outcomes toward majority positions rather than protecting minority views.
 - Institutional language is a major access barrier for democratic participation, with 92–96% of sentences from European Parliament and UN documents judged to require simplification in an evaluation, implying that AI readability tooling could materially expand who can engage meaningfully in policy processes.
 - Public legitimacy is a gating risk for AI-deliberation systems: in a 1,850-person German study, people were less willing to engage when AI facilitation was used (especially AI-sceptics), suggesting deployment can create a new participation divide even if output quality is high.

🎮 **Secure Coding Drift in LLM-Assisted Post-Quantum Cryptography Development: A Gamified Fix** [source](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.19474v1.pdf) #security `6/10` 

 *R. D. N. Shakya, C. P. Wijesiriwardana, S. M. Vidanagamachchi et al. (University of Moratuwa, University of Ruhuna, RMIT University)*

 The key idea is treating insecure LLM-assisted crypto coding as a behavioral drift problem and countering it with continuous, gamified security feedback loops embedded in the dev workflow.
 - Secure Coding Drift in PQC is framed as a longitudinal socio-technical vulnerability where repeated reliance on LLM-generated cryptographic code progressively reduces developer verification, undermining constant-time, side-channel resistance, randomness hygiene, and parameter discipline even when outputs pass functional tests.
 - The highest-risk failure mode is “silent insecurity,” where PQC implementations remain functionally correct while leaking through timing/side channels or misconfiguring parameters, implying that conventional unit testing and correctness checks are insufficient for AI-assisted PQC engineering workflows.
 - A proposed mitigation is a three-layer workflow (LLM code generation + hybrid security evaluation using static analysis and LLM-as-a-judge + gamified reinforcement) that adds drift indicators and behavior-linked scoring to incentivize auditing and rejection/fixing of insecure LLM suggestions rather than default acceptance.

🧬 **GAS-Leak-LLM: Genetic Algorithm-Based Suffix Optimization for Black-Box LLM Jailbreaking** [source](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.15788v1.pdf) #security `6/10` 

 *Aman Anifer, Vignesh Kumar Kembu, Vishnu M et al. (University of Pavia, Cochin University of Science and Technology)*

 A lightweight GA can reliably search the discrete prompt space in a strict black-box setting and still find transferable, semantically coherent suffixes that measurably degrade aligned-model refusal behavior.
 - A black-box genetic algorithm can evolve a universal adversarial suffix that raises Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct jailbreak rate from 5.96% (no suffix) to 17.76% (model-optimized suffix), showing that query-only optimization can materially erode instruction-tuned safety.
 - Model robustness varies drastically under the same evaluation: Qwen2.5-3B exhibits a 92.88% baseline jailbreak rate (fitness ≥ 0.6) while Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct is 5.96%, implying alignment quality dominates attack “headroom” and can dwarf gains from suffix optimization.
 - Semantically meaningful suffixes outperform meaningless ones on the more resistant model (Llama: 19.60% vs 11.96%), and truncating suffix length reduces success (18.36% to 13.20%), indicating linguistic coherence and longer control strings increase jailbreak leverage when baseline defenses are stronger.

🧩 **The Integrator Advantage: Controlled Agentic AI for Small and Medium-Sized Companies** [source](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.16649v1.pdf) #security `5/10` 

 *Christopner Koch, Joshua A. Wellbrock*

 The most defensible path to agentic AI value in SMEs is not “more autonomy,” but tighter integration: explicit use-case fit, bounded actions, measurable overrides, and auditable agent identities.
 - SMC adoption is rising but integration maturity lags, with 31% of SMCs across seven advanced economies using generative AI in late 2024 (Germany 39%) and 65% of adopters reporting improved employee performance without near-term staff reduction—implying the main bottleneck is operationalization rather than model access.
 - Controlled partial autonomy is positioned as the near-term sweet spot for SMCs, recommending autonomy Levels 2–4 (proposal → tool-using with approval → supervised standard-case automation) while explicitly avoiding full autonomy as a starting point due to verification and downside-risk constraints.
 - Identity, access control, and auditability are highlighted as gating requirements for production agents, reinforced by the claim that 68% of organizations cannot clearly distinguish human from AI-agent activity—implying agents need distinct non-human identities, least-privilege permissions, and kill switches to prevent over-privilege and untraceable actions.
